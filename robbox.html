Overview

Robbox has developed intelligent power tools for consumers and industrial applications to help us work smarter, safer, and more efficiently. After decades in the construction industry and realizing that power tools today have not caught up with the pace of new technology Robbox wanted to make a new generation of smart power tools.

Research & Problem Definition

1. Understand the Business

In this discovery phase, stakeholder interviews were conducted with the former CEO of Robbox and the Robbox development team to better understand the business goals and requirements. The problem I was able to solve was to translate their requirements into a list of features implemented into a simple prototype that we could test with users to determine whether these features were important. Also a feature analysis was conducted to determine the dificulty and impact of these features. Features included voice command, laser measurement, material selection, torque control, leveller guide, and stud-finder. The results of testing the prototype with users was insightful and some features were either eliminated or slighly changed to meet users requirements, which helped determine our MVP.

2. Understand the Users

Good research is fundamental to the success of the project. At the beginning, we interviewed two domain experts who were also our primary users. They were very excited about our project and were willing participants for our usability test later on. Key insights included learning about their wants, fears, and needs. Ultimately, they wanted a tool that was powerful and safe. They feared that an interactive screen would be distracting and vulnerable to damage. They needed a tool with useful features such as having a stud-finder and laser measurement.

3. Understand the Market

Market research showed that power tools including power devices in 2017 are a $51 billion dollar industry worldwide and expectected by 2025 to become a $100 billion dollar industry. Power drill only by 2015 is a $6 billion dollar industry Globally (USA - $3 Billion) and expected by 2025 to become a $12 billion dollar industry. Consumer Electronic Devices by 2024 are expected to hit $1400 billion globally. Robbox direct competitors include DeWalt, Hilti, and Milwaukee.

Design with the user in mind

This project is perhaps a world-first design solution for a power tool. My approach to designing was to follow John Maeda laws of simplicity. Law 1: the simplest way to achieve simplicity is through thoughtful reduction. Law 2: Organization makes a system of many appear fewer. With this in mind, every feature was simplified and users were able to navigate easily.

Project Learnings

Upon reflection, having great leadership with a strong and clear vision has ultimately made this project successful. Great communication with the CEO increased my productivity and produced favorable outcomes. It's essential to have clear and distinct roles as to not create conflict and roadblocks between team members. As a UX designer one of my many roles is to facilitate collaborative discussions with stakeholders and developers to get information about their business, users, and any technical contraints.
